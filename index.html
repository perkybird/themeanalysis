Watership Down Theme Analysis Essay
Word count: 700-900

Select a thematic topic from the text and develop a thematic statement.
• Power
• Freedom
• Exploration
• Your own choice

Thematic statement answers: What does Adams want us to know about __(thematic topic)___? or What is the universal idea, principle, or truth?
Literary device options include those we're discussed in class, but are not limited to, characterization, mood, allusion, foreshadowing, flashbacks, setting, imagery, conflict, symbolism, and irony. Consider also: figurative language, such as metaphor, and allegory.

Introduction:
• Lead/hook that introduces the theme. No rando websites. Give context and cite original source if you venture outside the novel.
• Summarize the story so that how Adams establishes the theme is clear.
• Explain how Adams uses the literary elements you chose.
• Build to your thesis, which should argue: Adams uses __literary elements__ to establish __thematic statement___.
• Note that the thesis is not the first place the reader should here about the theme or literary elements. A thesis should be a natural and smooth extension of the introduction.

Body paragraphs: 3-4
• Claims: directly prove the thesis, include a literary element and how it is used to establish the theme.
• Evidence*: quote, paraphrase, or summarize – cite.
• Analysis*: should be equal or longer in length to evidence. Explains how or why the evidence proves the claim.
• Concluding sentence

* You decide how much evidence and analysis this paragraph needs.

Conclusion:
• Summarize briefly, but reflect with insight
• Make an application: Why is Adam’s message important for children? For our current time? For believers?
